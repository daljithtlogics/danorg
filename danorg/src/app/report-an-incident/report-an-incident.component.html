PRIVACY

The Divers Alert Network (DAN) Research department will not share your information with any other department within DAN. DAN cares about your privacy and no individual shall be identified verbally or published. Data are released only in anonymous form and contact information of the reporting person will not be released publicly. Contact information is requested to facilitate follow-up if warranted (and if the person grants permission for contact to be made – asked as one of the final questions). All information obtained during the study, including the respondent’s identity, will be kept confidential to the full extent allowed by law.

ABOUT

We appreciate your time and effort in providing information about the diving incident and the results of our data collection and analysis may be made public for the benefit of our community. DAN (Divers Alert Network), with your help, has maintained a diving incident database since 1989. Originally, it was limited to scuba diving incidents. This online reporting system is designed to include a variety of incidents including breath-hold diving, rebreather diving, accidents and incidents that happened outside of the water.

INSTRUCTIONS

The incident collection instrument is divided into sections, each with a series of questions. The time required to complete the sections will vary with the amount of detail available and the complexity of the case. Each section will be saved automatically when you click “Continue”. After saving a page, you will automatically be sent to the next one. You will be able to move forward and backward through the pages to make any necessary changes until you click “Done” on the final page. At that time, you will no longer be able to return to the case. Please contact special projects@dansa.org if you have questions or issues concerning this process.

Description of the incident
In the following sections, please describe the incident to your best ability. You will find a list of keywords that might be helpful for you and for us to understand what circumstances may have been a factor in this incident, not all terms will apply to your case, you do not need to address all. 18. Document Upload (optional)
If you have already submitted the incident elsewhere (training agency, dive center, federal agencies, etc.) or have the description of the incident as a written document, you may upload the document here. Otherwise, please refer to the next sections to complete your report. Please note that this platform only supports PDF, DOC, DOCX, PNG, JPG, JPEG, and GIF files.
Report Sections
The following sections contain keywords as a guideline for you. Not all of them might be relevant to your incident, so you do not need to address all.
19. Section 1: Incident / Problem / Situation
1. What happened (brief description)?
2. Where did it happen (location, surface before the dive, during descent, at depth, during ascent, during deco, at the surface after the dive, out of the water)?
3. How many divers or non-divers were involved?
4. Number of divers affected by the incident?
5. Medical history/conditions?
20. Section 2: The Dive
Type of dive (training, recreational, technical, exploration, scientific, commercial, spearfishing/harvesting,...), preparation for the dive, type of equipment used, dive platform (e.g. boat, shore, pool), gas(es) used, altitude, max depth, weather/water conditions, time of day, etc.
21. Section 3: Outcome
1. What was the outcome of this incident?
2. Loss of consciousness, panic, out of gas emergency, separation, broken equipment, disorientation, emergency ascent, injury, fatality,...
22. Section 4: Emergency Response
1. Who if anyone responded to the incident underwater and/or at the surface (dive partner, first responder, boat crew, EMS, coast guard)?
2. What was done (share air, recovery, emergency ascent, first aid, emergency oxygen, equipment recovery, evacuation, hospitalization,...)?
23. Section 5: Contributing Factors
Address anything that might have contributed to the incident, e.g. (un)familiarity with equipment/dive site, diving experience level, task-loading, checklists used, weights carried, alcohol/lifestyle choices, haste, health problem/fitness to dive exam, fatigue, anxiety, training status, skill level, solo-dive, overhead environment, separation from dive partner, depth, visibility, temperature, decompression obligation, previous diving-related incidents etc.
24. Section 6: Lessons Learned (optional)
1. What can be learned from the incident and how could similar situations be prevented or handled better in the future?
2. What lessons would you like to share with divers reading this incident report?
25. Are you willing to talk to a member of the DAN team?
Are you willing to talk to a member of the DAN Team about this incident and to answer more questions to help us understand what may have contributed to the incident? Your contact information remains confidential and will not be shared.
26. Upload additional information (optional)
If you have additional documents you would like to share with us (incident reports submitted to agencies, police reports, news articles, dive profiles, photos, etc.) please upload those below. If the file format you want to upload is not supported, please attach the file to an email to specialprojects@dansa.org.
